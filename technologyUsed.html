Technology Used

Phycoremediation

Phycoremediation is defined as the use of either macro-algae or micro-algae (Using Sun Light and Carbon Di Oxide) for the removal or biotransformation of pollutants, including nutrients and Xenobiotic from polluted Water. Green Micro-Algae carries out Photosynthesis using Sun Light and absorbing Carbon Di-Oxide. And it also helps in Purification of the Air too thus reducing Carbon Foot Prints

Our team will conduct a thorough analysis of the effluent/ river / lake and will identify the select strains of micro-algae that are capable of growing and remediating these streams. Simultaneously, the micro-nutrients will be identified that will be able to trigger the growth of a healthy “micro-algae – bacteria” consortium in the canals. Micro-algae never survive on their own. They are always associated in a symbiotic relationship with a host of bacterial species. Our technology lies in identifying the right combination of micro-algae, bacteria and micronutrient that can give the best optimized solution for a particular problem.

A stabilized culture of isolated micro-algae – bacteria consortium will be grown and developed at our manufacturing facility.
